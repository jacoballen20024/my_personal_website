Blog Post

My journey to becoming a web developer started with my fiancée. I worked at Food Lion as a cashier, then a customer lead, and then was promoted to Front End Office Assistant which meant I was responsible for counting the safe, loaning and picking up cash, running the customer service desk, sending people to luch on time, basically I was in charge of the front end of the store each evening and had to make good judgement calls in a fast paced busy location. I was nearing completion of my associates degree pursuing a career in biomedical engineering, work was close to home and the schedule was flexible until I worked in the office. I eventually left to work for Amazon because I had heard how much better it was from my friends at work who had quit to work there. I was especially interested once they told me Amazon pays for your school. I initially planned on using that for my degree, but decided to try a quicker route looking into what was offered. When I saw software development on the list I knew it was what I wanted. I want to get started in a career that pays well so I can provide for my wife and kids, and I want it to be something that I can do from anywhere so I can spend more quality time with family, as well as being a career that doesn't put so much stress on my body.

So far I've learned a lot about software development. I have no previous experience, so at times I've felt a little overwhelmed but I also appreciate the fast pace. I want a career ASAP so I can marry my fiancée. She is my greatest motivation and this is worth every sleepless night, all the studying, and every frustration I run into with my code. I've learned that HTML can work without CSS but CSS cannot work without HTML, what tags are and how to use them, how to format my webpage, and so much more. What I've found most interesting though has been CSS. I really enjoy making things look appealing and easy to read and navigate.
